Understanding Liquid Argon Quantities

dE/dx: Energy loss per unit distance. For minimum ionizing particles (MIP), typically ~2.12 MeV/cm in LAr.
Range: Distance a particle travels before stopping, depends on initial energy and particle type.
Ionization Yield: Number of electron-ion pairs produced per MeV of deposited energy. W-value ~23.6 eV means ~42,000 e⁻/MeV.
Scintillation Yield: Number of VUV photons (128 nm) produced per MeV. LAr produces ~40,000 photons/MeV.
Recombination: Fraction of ionization electrons that recombine before collection. Electric field dependent (~30% loss at 500 V/cm for MIPs).
Quenching Factor: Ratio of light/charge yield for nuclear recoils vs. electron recoils. Typically ~0.25-0.7 for nuclear recoils.
Radiation Length: Distance over which a high-energy electron loses all but 1/e of its energy by bremsstrahlung. ~14 cm in LAr.
Nuclear Recoil: Energy transferred to argon nuclei in elastic scattering. Important for neutron and dark matter detection.
Mean Free Path: Average distance a particle travels before interacting. Varies by particle type and energy.
Cross Section: Effective area for interaction. Neutrinos have tiny cross sections (~10⁻³⁸ cm² at GeV scale).
